Titi Zenny's Famous Coquito

My Titi Zenny's coquito is a staple within the Rodriguez/Carri√≥n family. A Puerto Rican take on the holidays' most beloved beverage, egg nog, the sweet, delicate coconut mixed with cinnamon masks the obscene amount of rum present, serving a rude reminder when you're already on your third cup.

My titi, or my aunt, taught me this recipe when I was living in Scotland. While she sells branded bottles all over the Latin-infused streets of Bushwick, Brooklyn, I used the coquito to impress my Scottish and British friends, often being lauded for being able to keep up with the Scots' drinking. Plus, I can't really cook so this was my contribution to our Friendsgiving.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans of condensed milk
  • 2 cans of Carnation evaporated milk
  • 1 can of creme of coconut
  • 1 can of coconut milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon of cinnamon
  • 2 cups of silver rum (I usually just do Bacardi)
  • Optional: 1/2 teaspoon of cardamom
  • Soak the rum with cinnamon sticks for 24 hours prior to making the coquito
    1. Blend all the ingredients
    2. Let chill for minimum 4 hours before serving (I recommend 24 hours)
